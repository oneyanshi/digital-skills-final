Mightier Than Swords holds their first open mic night of the year

Sept. 24, 2015. By Yan Shi.

A full house at Davenport Coffee Lounge.

Several American University students gathered outside the Davenport Coffee Lounge for the first open mic this year held by the university’s slam poetry club, Mightier Than Swords.

The evening began with a late start as the coffee shop was closing up for the night and could not allow people to come in until the shop was cleaned up. The lively, excited group of students waited outside, chattered among friends and strangers and signed up to perform during the wait.

The students eventually migrated inside. They occupied the space from wall to wall.The dimly lit room was bursting at the seams. Chairs had to be brought in from outside to accompany those who wanted seating.

E-board member Ryan Shepard was overjoyed to see the number of people that attended the open mic.

“This is a great turnout for us. Last year, I think, five or six people were at the club and it’s really nice to see you all here tonight,” he said.

Students performed poems on topics ranging from love to mental illnesses to race to letters to Beyoncé. From lighthearted to dark, the room’s mood changed frequently as new voices came to the front of the audience eager to devour poetry.

The small coffee shop was almost never silent. There would always be a few snaps during a performance. Claps filled the room at the end of each poem.

“If you’re vibing with a poem or what the poet is saying,” said Emma-Claire Martin, another Mightier than Swords e-board member, “you know, snap. Speak up. Be vocal about it.”


Emma-Claire Martin reciting poetry.

The encouragement, the acceptance and the broad range of topics encouraged shyer attendees to perform their own poetry as well. One in particular came up to the front after support from the e-board members and the crowd. She was soft-spoken, but there was a firmness to her voice.

“I wanted to try and be a little bit braver,” the student told the audience. “So I wrote a quick poem because everybody…was really brave. I wanted to be more like the people who came up here.”

Resounding snaps, claps and cheers erupted from the crowd before quieting down again to let the student perform.

The variety of voice styles and body language was as diverse as the crowd itself, ranging from freshman to juniors to slam poetry novices to experts. The environment was welcoming and warm. Many left the coffee shop feeling satisfied and glad that they attended.

The American University slam team has gone to several competitions since it was established. Last year, they were ranked 23rd out of 68 at the 2015 College Unions Poetry Slam Invitational (CUPSI). The team announced that they intend to compete in a slam poetry competition in Texas in the spring.

Members of the club also mentioned that they will be organizing writing workshops and other meetings throughout the year. More about the team and the events that will be held can be found through their Twitter, Instagram, or Facebook page.
